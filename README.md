# MartEdge Retail Group — Power BI Analytics Dashboard

## Background
MartEdge Retail Group is an omnichannel retail company that launched 
in July 2019 with 3 pilot branches across Lagos, Abuja and Rivers. 
By 2022 the company had expanded to 10 branches across 8 Nigerian 
states — Lagos, Abuja, Rivers, Kano, Delta, Enugu, Edo and Osun,
selling across 7 product categories through both physical stores and 
online channels.

Despite recording ₦825M in revenue and growing 27% year on year, 
the CFO raised a concern that nobody could immediately explain,
profit margin was declining. The CEO commissioned a full analytics 
solution before the Q1 2026 board meeting to identify the root cause 
and provide actionable recommendations.

---

## Business Objective
Identify the root causes of margin decline across all 10 branches 
between July 2019 and February 2026, determine which customer 
segments, product categories and store locations are driving or 
destroying value, and provide actionable recommendations to recover 
profitability in 2026.

---

## Dashboard Pages

### Page 1 — Executive Overview
*A snapshot of revenue growth, profitability and overall business health*

Covers total revenue, profit and margin vs last year, month-on-month 
trend table with running totals and rolling 3-month average, revenue 
and profit by year, profit vs cost trend, revenue by sales channel 
and payment method and seasonal sales volume analysis.

**Key Finding:** Revenue grew 27% to ₦825M but profit margin dropped 
1.2% vs last year. Costs have been rising faster than revenue since 
the June 2023 naira devaluation and fuel subsidy removal, revenue 
growth is masking a profitability crisis.

![Executive Overview](images/overview.png)

---

### Page 2 — Customer Insights
*Understanding who the customers are and how they interact with the business*

Covers repeat purchase rate, churn rate, AOV, total customers vs 
last year, revenue by loyalty status, customer distribution by gender, 
revenue and profit by state, revenue by age group, AOV and purchase 
frequency by segment and customer base growth over time.

**Key Finding:** Loyalty members are only 37% of the customer base 
but drive 52.7% of total revenue, each loyalty customer generates 
2.7x more value than a non-loyalty customer. The 46-60 age group 
is the highest spending segment at ₦214M. Churn improved by 6.5% 
year on year.

![Customer Insights](images/customers.png)

---

### Page 3 — Product Performance
*What is selling, what is profitable and what is quietly losing money*

Covers revenue and profit margin by category, average discount by 
category, top performing products by revenue and margin, revenue 
by sub-category and total quantity sold by sub-category.

**Key Finding:** Electronics dominates revenue at ₦343M but Home 
Appliances profit margin has collapsed. The LG NeoChef is operating 
at just 6.1% margin while recording high sales volume, a supplier 
cost problem that is quietly destroying value.

![Product Performance](images/products.png)

## Key Findings

- Revenue grew 27% but profit margin declined 1.2% vs last year,
  costs rising faster than revenue since June 2023 inflation shock
- Loyalty members are 37% of customers but drive 52.7% of revenue which means 
  each loyalty customer is worth 2.7x more than a non-loyalty customer
- The 46-60 age group generates the most revenue at ₦214M — an 
  undermarketed segment with significant untapped potential
- LG NeoChef operating at 6.1% margin, Home Appliances selling well 
  but losing money
- Churn rate improved 6.5% year on year,  retention strengthening 
  despite economic pressure
- Delta State leads all 8 states at ₦151M , outperforming larger 
  markets including Lagos and Abuja

---

## Recommendations

1. Renegotiate Home Appliances supplier contracts, the current cost 
   structure makes profitable selling impossible at market price
2. Reprice low-margin SKUs, products operating below 15% margin 
   need immediate review
3. Invest in loyalty programme marketing, loyalty members deliver 
   2.7x more revenue per customer and repeat purchase rate is at 
   64% and growing
4. Target the 46-60 age segment, highest spending group with no 
   dedicated marketing strategy currently
5. Investigate discount approval patterns, one employee at the 
   Lagos Lekki branch approving discounts at 18% vs company 
   average of 4%
